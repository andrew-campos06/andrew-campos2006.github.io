    About Me

    Andrew Campos

    Mechanical Engineer | Leader | Pilot | Aerospace/Robotics Enthusiast

    I'm a Mechanical Engineering student from Palo Alto, CA, entering my sophomore year at Columbia University in New York City. My interests center on robotics, autonomous systems, and aerospace technologies—fields I've explored through both research and hands-on work. Currently, I’m interning at NASA’s Ames Research Center and contributing to the development of a custom PairTilt quadcopter in UC Berkeley’s High Performance Robotics Lab. I’ve also grown overtime through leadership—serving as class president for two years and participating in my school’s leadership program throughout high school. Whether in the classroom, on the field, or in a team environment, I value collaboration and clear communication. At heart, I’m driven by a desire to build and problem-solve. I enjoy tackling abstract challenges with creativity and precision, and I’m always looking for new ways to apply engineering to real-world problems.
